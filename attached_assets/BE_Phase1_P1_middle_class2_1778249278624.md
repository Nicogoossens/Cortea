# === Q1: The Initial Contact ===
question_text: |
  You are emailing a potential collaborator for the first time. Which
  greeting best balances respect and approachability?

    answer_tier: 1
    motivation: |
      Della Casa's register-discipline: the greeting must match the act.
      "Dear" anchors formality; the warm purpose modulates it. The
      calibrated middle-class register — neither falsely peer-near nor
      falsely senior-distant.
  - text: "\"Hi [Name], I'm reaching out because I saw your work and wanted to chat.\""
    answer_tier: 2
    motivation: |
      Hi/chat fits a known peer; for first contact it reads as register-
      light. The writer has not paused to choose the register the
      relationship has not yet earned. Workable after rapport, premature
      here.

    answer_tier: 3
    motivation: |

question_text: |
  You notice you misspelled a client's name in a previous email. How do
  you fix it?
options:
  - text: "Send a quick follow-up: \"Apologies for the typo in your name, [Correct Name]. I've corrected my records.\""
    answer_tier: 1
    motivation: |
      Goffman's face-work: the misspelled name is a small face-slight.
      Specific, brief acknowledgement repairs without inflating. Goleman's
      trained-competence form — own the slip, name the correction, move
      on.
  - text: "Ignore it and just spell it correctly next time."
    answer_tier: 2
    motivation: |
      Silent correction avoids awkwardness but leaves the slight
      unrepaired. If the client registered it, the silent fix reads as
      oversight or avoidance. Workable for low-stakes contacts; under-
      investing for a client.

    answer_tier: 3
    motivation: |
      Over-explanation inverts the face-work — the apology now occupies

      writer's mental state. The Bescheiden register specifically resists
      self-centred apology-inflation.
