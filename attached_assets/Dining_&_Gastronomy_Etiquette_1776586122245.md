# Database Schema: Dining & Gastronomy Etiquette

## 1. Utensils & Handling
- **ID:** `DIN_001`
- **Field: Primary_Tools**
  - *Values:* Cutlery (Western), Chopsticks (Asia), Hands (Right hand only?), Bread as a tool.
- **Field: Cutlery_Language**
  - *Values:* Position of knife/fork to signal "finished" vs. "pause".
- **Field: Finger_Food_Boundaries**
  - *Values:* What is acceptable to touch (e.g., pizza in Italy vs. asparagus in certain circles).

## 2. Consumption Habits
- **ID:** `DIN_002`
- **Field: Noise_Norms**
  - *Values:* Slurping (compliment in Japan) vs. Total silence (Western formal).
- **Field: Plate_Cleaning**
  - *Values:* Finish everything (respect) vs. Leave a small bite (sign of abundance/host's generosity).
- **Field: Serving_Order**
  - *Values:* Eldest first, guest of honor first, or women first.

## 3. Drinking & Toasting
- **ID:** `DIN_003`
- **Field: Refill_Policy**
  - *Values:* Self-service vs. Never pour your own drink (Japan/Korea).
- **Field: Toasting_Hierarchy**
  - *Values:* Who initiates? Eye contact rules during "Cheers".
- **Field: Alcohol_Expectations**
  - *Values:* Mandatory participation vs. Easy opt-out for tea/water.

## 4. Seating & Positioning
- **ID:** `DIN_004`
- **Field: Power_Seat**
  - *Values:* Location of the host relative to the door or the view.
- **Field: Gender_Seating**
  - *Values:* Mixed seating vs. Separated by gender/status.

## 5. Financials & Tipping
- **ID:** `DIN_005`
- **Field: Tipping_Standard**
  - *Values:* Percentage, "Rounding up", or "Tipping is an insult" (e.g., Japan/Iceland).
- **Field: The_Bill_Dance**
  - *Values:* Fighting for the bill (Middle East/China) vs. Split-the-bill (Netherlands/Germany).
